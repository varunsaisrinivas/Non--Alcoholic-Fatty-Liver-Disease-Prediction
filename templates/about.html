About NAFLD

NAFLD stands for Non-Alcoholic fatty Liver Disease

Non-Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD) Overview :

NAFLD is a common liver condition associated with obesity and diabetes. It develops when excess fat accumulates in the liver without alcohol consumption.

Symptoms and Progression :

Initially, NAFLD may be asymptomatic, but it can progress, leading to fatigue and abdominal discomfort.

Diagnosis :

Diagnosis involves a thorough medical history, specific tests, and imaging studies.

Management through Lifestyle Changes :

Lifestyle changes, such as adopting a healthy diet and incorporating regular exercise, are crucial for managing NAFLD.

Importance of Early Detection :

Early detection and intervention are essential for improving outcomes and preventing severe complications.

Research and Support :

The National Institute of Diabetes and Digestive and Kidney Diseases (NIDDK) and other components of the National Institutes of Health (NIH) conduct research and provide support for various diseases and conditions.
